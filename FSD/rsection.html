Indus Valley Civilization: This sophisticated urban civilization, flourishing between 3300 and 1300 BCE, is one of the earliest in the world, characterized by advanced city planning, trade, and agricultural practices. Vedic Period: Following the decline of the Indus Valley Civilization, the Indo-Aryan people migrated to the region, leading to the development of the Vedic period and its religious and cultural traditions. Mauryan Empire: Chandragupta Maurya unified much of the Indian subcontinent, creating a powerful empire that oversaw significant advancements in art, architecture, and governance. Gupta Empire: This empire, known as a "golden age" of Indian history, saw significant advancements in science, mathematics, literature, and art.
